# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Software engineers, testers, team leads, and engineering managers who want an honest picture of where their test automation practice actually stands. They're typically hands-on practitioners or the people responsible for them. They arrive skeptical of hype and impatient with vague advice — they want a straight answer and a clear next step.

## Product Purpose

A public-facing maturity assessment built on Bas Dijkstra's Valuable Feedback, Fast framework. Visitors answer 36 questions across 8 dimensions and receive a scored result with a maturity level, a breakdown by dimension, and concrete next steps. The site also surfaces training resources and a path to contact Bas directly. Success means a visitor finishes the assessment with a clearer sense of where they stand and what to do about it.

## Brand Personality

Direct, warm, frank. The voice of a test automation consultant who has seen too many teams pick the wrong tool for the wrong reasons — and who is genuinely on the reader's side. Humour is earned, not performed. Advice is concrete, not hedged. The reader is treated as a professional who can handle the truth.

## Anti-references

None specified.

## Design Principles

- **Straight answers over polish.** The assessment is the product. Every design choice should make it easier to engage with the content, not draw attention to itself.
- **Warmth without softness.** Approachable tone and visual friendliness should never tip into vagueness or corporate comfort. The site should feel like a trusted colleague, not a brochure.
- **Progress feels earned.** The assessment is 36 questions. The design should make that feel manageable — visible progress, clear structure, a payoff worth reaching.
- **Hierarchy earns trust.** A clear visual hierarchy signals that the person behind this knows what they're talking about. Clutter and inconsistency undermine the message before a word is read.
- **Mobile is a first-class context.** Engineers assess their teams on their laptops, but they share links and check results on their phones. Everything must work at both.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. No specific known user needs beyond standard compliance. Reduced-motion alternatives required for any animations.
