The Test Automation Quadrant

Not all tests deserve to exist — and this model makes that uncomfortably clear. The Test Automation Quadrant plots every test on two axes: information value and efficiency. The result is a straightforward framework for deciding what to invest in, what to tolerate despite the cost, what to deprioritize, and what to delete without a second thought. Worth a read even if you think your suite is in good shape.

Course

Valuable Feedback, Fast (for engineers)

Three days of deliberate work on why your test automation does or doesn't deliver — and what to do about it. This isn't a tool tutorial. It covers strategy, risk-based coverage, test design, pipeline thinking, and how to leave with an actual plan rather than just a good feeling. Available in Java, C#, and TypeScript. Best for engineers and testers who are ready to move past "which framework should we use" and start asking better questions.

Valuable Feedback, Fast — Applied (for teams)

This one is specifically for teams — meaning it works on your challenges, not a fictional case study. Over two days, you'll map your actual feedback loops, interrogate your test design choices, and figure out together what needs to change. You leave with a shared plan, not a slide deck nobody looks at again. Designed for teams of 6–25 who are ready to have the honest conversations that most teams quietly avoid.
